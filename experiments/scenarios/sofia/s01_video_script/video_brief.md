# Video Brief — "AI Tools That Actually Changed How I Work"

## Concept
Honest, personal take on AI tools I use daily as a content creator.
Not a sponsored tier list — genuine stuff that made a difference.
Want it to feel like talking to a friend, not a tech review channel.

## Target length
10–12 minutes (script ~1500–1800 words)

## My usual video structure
1. Hook — personal moment or question (30–45 sec)
2. Quick context — who this is for, why I'm making it (30 sec)
3. Main content — 4–5 tools, each gets a story moment + practical tip
4. Honest caveat — what AI still can't do for me
5. Call to action — newsletter + comment question

## Tools I actually want to cover
- ChatGPT / Claude — for scripting, brainstorming, repurposing
- Canva AI — magic resize, text-to-image for thumbnails
- CapCut AI — auto captions, background remover
- Notion AI — organising content calendar, drafting briefs
- ElevenLabs — experimented with voiceover (mixed feelings)

## Tone reference
Think: cosy Sunday morning, not a startup pitch.
Warm, a bit self-deprecating, honest about failures.
I swear occasionally (mildly — fine for my audience).

## What I want to avoid
- Sounding like I'm reading a script (it should feel improvised even though it isn't)
- Tech jargon my audience won't know
- Overselling — my audience trusts me because I'm honest about limitations
