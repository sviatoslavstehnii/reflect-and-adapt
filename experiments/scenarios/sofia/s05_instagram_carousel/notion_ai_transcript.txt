okay so I've been using Notion AI every single day for three months now and I genuinely have
mixed feelings. Like, I wanted to love it. I really did. And some of it I do love. But some
of it is just... not what I expected? So let me just be really honest with you about all of it.

First — the thing I use most. Every single morning I open Notion and I use the AI to help me
draft my content brief for whatever I'm filming that week. And it's actually really good at this.
I give it like a rough idea, a few bullet points of what I want to cover, and it fills in the
gaps, suggests a structure, sometimes catches things I forgot to include. That part? Ten out of
ten. It's saved me probably an hour a week just on planning.

The second thing — and this is where it gets a bit complicated — is the writing assistant.
So you can highlight any text in Notion and ask it to improve it, summarise it, make it longer,
shorter, change the tone. And honestly this is hit or miss. When I ask it to make something
more casual it usually nails it. But when I ask it to be more professional? It goes full
corporate robot mode. Like, suddenly I'm "leveraging synergies" and I'm like... that's not
a human. So you have to be really specific about what you want.

The third thing I've started using it for is organising my video ideas backlog. I have like
sixty ideas in there and I asked Notion AI to group them by theme and priority and it did
a genuinely good job. Took it about ten seconds. Would have taken me twenty minutes manually.
So for organisation tasks? Really solid.

Now the stuff that disappointed me. The Q&A feature — where you can ask Notion questions about
your own workspace — is supposed to be this incredible thing. And in theory it's amazing. But
in practice? It's kind of slow. And it sometimes pulls from the wrong page. Like I asked it
about my filming schedule and it gave me information from a page I archived months ago. So
I wouldn't rely on it for anything important yet.

The other thing is the AI database features — like the AI autofill columns — those are still
pretty rough. They look impressive in demos but in real use they're inconsistent. I tried using
it to auto-tag my video ideas by topic and it got maybe 60% of them right. Not bad, but not
good enough to trust blindly.

So is it worth the extra cost? If you're already a heavy Notion user — yes, probably. If you're
only using Notion casually, maybe wait. For me it's become part of my actual workflow which
means I'll keep paying for it. But I went in expecting magic and what I got was more like a
really useful assistant who's great at some things and a bit unreliable at others. Which is
kind of just... AI in 2026, honestly.

Anyway that's my take after three months. Let me know in the comments if you use Notion AI
and whether you're getting more out of it than I am — genuinely curious.
